Namma Yatri — Complete Product Overview
========================================

What is Namma Yatri?
--------------------
Namma Yatri is an open-source, zero-commission ride-hailing application built on India's Open
Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC) protocol. It was developed by Juspay Technologies in
collaboration with the Government of Karnataka and the Bengaluru Metropolitan Transport
Corporation (BMTC).

The name "Namma Yatri" means "Our Commuter" in Kannada, reflecting its community-driven ethos.

Zero Commission Model
---------------------
Unlike Ola and Uber that charge 20–30% commission per ride, Namma Yatri operates on a
subscription model. Drivers pay a fixed weekly fee (approximately ₹25–50/week) and keep
100% of every fare they earn. This is the platform's most powerful differentiator.

Impact: A driver doing 25 rides/day at ₹150 average fare earns ₹3,750/day. With Ola/Uber
at 25% commission, they would lose ₹937/day or ~₹28,000/month. Namma Yatri saves drivers
this entire amount.

Open Source
-----------
The complete Namma Yatri codebase is available on GitHub under the Apache 2.0 license.
This enables:
- Community contributions and audits
- Third-party integrations
- Trust through transparency
- No vendor lock-in

ONDC Protocol
-------------
Built on the Open Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC), Namma Yatri is interoperable
with other ONDC-compliant apps. Riders on any ONDC app can book Namma Yatri drivers,
and vice versa. This open ecosystem approach prevents monopoly formation.

Competitive Positioning
-----------------------
Namma Yatri vs Ola:
- Commission: 0% vs 20–25%
- Model: Subscription vs Per-ride cut
- Pricing: Market-determined vs Surge pricing
- Philosophy: Driver-first vs Investor-first
- Code: Open source vs Proprietary

Namma Yatri vs Uber:
- Commission: 0% vs 25–30%
- Coverage: Regional (South India) vs National/Global
- Support: Community-driven vs Corporate
- Pricing: Transparent vs Dynamic surge

Namma Yatri vs Rapido:
- Vehicle type: Autos & Cabs vs Bikes & Autos
- Commission: 0% vs 15–20%
- ONDC: Yes vs No

Technology Stack
----------------
Frontend: React Native (Android & iOS)
Backend: Haskell (Beckn/ONDC protocol layer), Node.js microservices
Database: PostgreSQL, Redis
Infrastructure: AWS (EC2, S3, RDS)
Maps: Google Maps API
Payments: Juspay (UPI aggregator)
Protocol: Beckn Protocol 1.1 (ONDC)

Pain Points Reported (User Research)
-------------------------------------
Rider Pain Points:
1. Long wait times during off-peak hours
2. No advance/scheduled booking option
3. Driver cancellations after acceptance
4. Limited cab availability (mostly autos)
5. No in-app customer support chat
6. App crashes on low-end Android devices
7. No bill/invoice generation for corporate users

Driver Pain Points:
1. Low ride density in suburban areas
2. No heat map showing demand zones
3. App battery drain during long shifts
4. Limited earnings analytics
5. No insurance or benefits program
6. Difficulty with non-tech-savvy drivers using app
